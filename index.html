Cyber-physical and Cybersecurity

  • Farah Kandah, PhD

    I am an IEEE Senior Member and Tenured Associate Professor in Computer Science and Software Engineering (CSSE) at the Auburn University .

    My research is primarily focused on developing robust cybersecurity methodologies, including the creation of advanced schemes, techniques, algorithms, and solutions specifically tailored to enhance computer network security. A key aspect of my work is to identify, counteract, and mitigate various forms of cyber threats and malicious attacks, thereby fortifying the network's defenses against potential security breaches.

    I am the founder and the lead director of the Network communication Laboratory (NCL) that leverages expertise in cyber-phsical systems, cybersecurity, smart communication, threat hunting, Blockchain, and trust management with research focuses on urban science, Internet of Things, public safety, smart networking design, smart autonomous/connected vehicle networks, and Software-Defined Networks.
